Flu Prevention

A small step for you,
a giant leap for the community.

Flu spreads without your notice. Flu can be spread from person to person by the means of droplets, including coughing, sneezing, and even talking. In some cases, touching infectious droplets on surfaces then touching nose and mouth can also increase risk of flu infection.

Wash your hands. Washing hands after touching objects can effectively prevent flu infection and other diseases, as the viruses are removed from your hands.


Wear medical masks. Medical masks can serve as a mutual protection. Masks can block droplets from others from contacting your nose and mouths, decreasing possibility of flu infection. They can also prevent others from getting your droplets, protecting the health of others.

Get flu vaccination. Flu shot can effectively prevent flu infection and reduce any symptom if infected. It can also prevent hospitalization caused by flu complication for high risk populations such as seniors, infants, and pregnant women.
